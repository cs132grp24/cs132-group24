Lift Our project entitled Iangat ang Buhay na Katotohanan aims to investigate mis/disinformation tweets in Twitter on the alleged alliance of Angat Buhay Foundation to the CPP-NPA-NDF.
Data Science Team:
Inah Janin Raymart

Here's an overview of our project.

Motivation

In today's interconnected world, social media platforms like Twitter have become powerful tools for communication, information sharing, and influence. However, they have also become breeding grounds for the rapid dissemination of false and misleading information.

Problem

The increasing prevalence of social media mis/disinformation, with its alarming spread by media influencers and even political figures, poses a significant threat to the public perception of Angat Buhay Foundation along with its mission, efforts, and intentions.

Solution

We aim to utilize data science techniques to understand social media dynamics, identify patterns, and contribute to a nuanced understanding of the impact of mis/disinformation on public perception of Angat Buhay.

We defined the problem and formulated a hypothesis.

Research Question

What were the characteristics of the mis/disinformation tweets in terms of:

    a. the account type of the authors/posters,

    b. the topics or themes covered in the tweet content,

    c. the timeframe the tweets were posted?

Hypothesis

a. Anonymous accounts are more likely to spread mis/disinformation about Angat Buhay's alleged alliance

   with the NPA compared to non-anonymous accounts.

b. Analysis of topic clusters in tweets about Angat Buhay's alliance with the NPA reveals dominant narratives,

   including the red tagging of Leni Robredo.

c. There is a significant increase in tweets before and after Angat Buhay Foundation's official launch.

Null Hypothesis

a. Anonymous and non-anonymous accounts are equally likely to spread mis/disinformation about Angat Buhay's

   alleged alliance with the NPA.

b. Dominant narratives in the topic clusters do not include the red tagging of Leni Robredo.

c. There is no significant increase in tweets before and after Angat Buhay Foundation's official launch.

Action Plan

Analyze Twitter accounts that posted mis/disinformation about Angat Buhay being allied with the CPP-NPA-NDF.

Data Pre-processing
We applied multiple pre-processing techniques to clean our dataset.

Handling missing values
Missing values can lead to inaccurate or biased results thus appropriate handling is necessary.
 

  • The missing values in the Account bio and Location columns were filled with an empty string.

Categorical data encoding
To prepare the data for modelling, some features need to be transformed into their correct data type.
 

  • To use the Date posted column for time series analysis, its data type is converted from object to datetime.

  • The data type of Joined column is also converted from object to period.

  • The Account type column is a categorical feature thus its data type is converted from object to category.

  • One-hot encoding is a process where categorical variables are converted in a format that is interpretable by machine learning algorithms. The Tweet type (Text, Image, Video, URL, Reply) and Content type (Rational, Emotional, Transactional) columns are multicategorical features that need to be one-hot encoded in order for their values to be used for tweet analysis. For example, if a record's Tweet type contains "Text, Image, Reply", it will be converted into (1, 1, 0, 0, 1). We used sklearn's MultiLabelBinarizer to generate new columns denoting the multiple labels of each sample.

Ensuring formatting consistency
We made sure that all variables are consistently written. This is especially important for columns containing dates and numbers.
 

  • The consistency of the dates found in the Joined and Date posted columns were checked and corrected manually. To further ensure that the formatting in all rows is correct, we used Python's datetime module.

  • The numbers in the Following and Followers columns were also converted from string to integer.

Detecting outliers
Outliers are data points that deviate from the expected distribution. It is important to identify them in order to understand why they occur and to decide how to best handle them.
 

  • We looked for potential outliers in the following columns: Joined, Date posted, Followers, Likes, Retweets, Quote tweets, and Replies.

Normalizing the data
Normalization is done by adjusting the data of numerical categories to a common scale.
 

  • We applied normalization to the following numerical columns: Followers, Likes, Retweets, Quote tweets, and Replies.

Data binning
This is the process of dividing a continuous variable into a set of discrete intervals.
 

  • We grouped the dates in the Date posted column into monthly, daily, and hourly bins in preparation for time series analysis.

Generating features
This is the process of creating new features from one or more existing features in the dataset.
 

  • We created a new feature called Tweet length from Tweet translated.

Natural Language Processing
We applied multiple NLP techniques to aid us in identifying recurrent topics in the tweets of our dataset.

Manual Pre-processing
The following pre-processing techniques were manually applied to the Tweet column.
 

  • Translation of tweets to English. Translated tweets were put in a new column: Tweet Translated
  • Removal of image alt text descriptions.
  • Conversion of emojis to descriptive text.

Removal of Special Characters and Tokenization
Tokenization is the process of splitting a piece of text into smaller units of information called tokens.
 

  • We remove special characters (":", "@", "#", punctuation marks) from the translated tweets.
  • Before tokenization, we converted the translated tweets into lowercase and also removed the stopwords.
  • We applied tokenization to the translated tweets using NLTK's word_tokenize function. A new column called texts_tok is created to store the list of tokens of each tweet.

Stemming
Stemming is the process of reducing a word to its word stem by removing its affixes.
 

  • We applied stemming to the translated tweets using NLTK's PorterStemmer function. A new column called texts_stem is created to store the list of stemmed words of each tweet.

Lemmatization
Lemmatization is the process of grouping together inflected forms of a word. It uses a dictionary (WordNet) to carry out morphological analysis of the words.
 

  • We applied lemmatization to the translated tweets using NLTK's WordNetLemmatizer function. A new column called texts_lem is created to store the list of lemmatized words of each tweet.

Let's uncover insights, patterns, and trends in the data to answer the research problem.

Data modeling is the process of creating a visual representation or model of data elements to understand how they relate to one another. A model is a representation of real-world data. Our data modeling process involves statistical modeling and machine learning.

Statistical Modeling
We used a statistical test to test our hypothesis on the characteristic of mis/disinformation tweets in terms of the account type of the authors.

Chi-square Goodness of Fit Test

We utilized the Chi-square goodness of fit test on a dataset of 151 tweets. This non-parametrical test assumes independence of samples. Our expected frequency was set at 75.5 which is 50% of our total records.

Figure #19: Bar graph of observed and expected frequencies of account types.

Null hypothesis: The distribution of anonymous and identified accounts is equal, following a 50/50 distribution.
 
Alternative hypothesis: The distribution of anonymous and identified accounts deviated from a 50/50 distribution.
 
Significance level (p-value): 0.05
Confidence level: 95%
Degree of freedom: 1
 
Limitation: For this test, we only assumed that the accounts in our dataset are independent samples based on the fact that each account corresponds to a unique tweet. Independence testing is needed to ensure independence of samples.

Result

Our statistical test indicated a significant difference in the distribution of anonymous and identified accounts. With an obtained Chi-square statistic of 96.96, surpassing the critical value of 3.641, we reject the null hypothesis.
This suggests that anonymous accounts are more likely to spread fake news about Angat Buhay Foundation's alleged alliance with the CPP-NPA-NDF.

Machine Learning
For computational modeling, we decided to build two machine learning models: a topic clustering model and an event detection model to give light to our hypotheses on the underlying themes of the tweets as well as the timeframe the tweets were posted.

Topic Clustering

Pre-processing

For topic clustering, we used the translated version of the tweet contents from the Tweet Translated column. The following pre-processing techniques (as discussed in the Data section) were applied to the tweet content:

  • Removal of stop words and special characters
  • Conversion of emojis to descriptive text
  • Tokenization
  • Stemming
  • Lemmatization

Another pre-processing step was applied where the following similar terms were replaced in order to consolidate them under a single word:

  • Words containing "pinklawan" or "kultongpinklawan" were replaced with the term "dilawan"
  • Words containing "npa" were replaced with the term "npa"

Lastly, the tweet contents were processed using TF-IDF. TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) is a commonly used natural language processing technique to represent text data as numerical features. It helps capture the importance of each word in a document within a collection of documents.

Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)

Latent Dirichlet Allocation is an unsupervised topic modeling technique that can classify text from a document to specific topics. In our case, we used it to classify text from tweet contents in order to extract five topics.

After obtaining the numerical feature vectors from TF-IDF, we used LDA to discover the latent topics in the collection by fitting and transforming the vectors.

  • Fitting - refers to providing the input data to the LDA model and estimating the topic proportions for each tweet
  • Transforming - refers to converting the new data into a topic vector representation based on the learned model

LDA has two outputs:

  • The top keywords per topic, in our case the model will output five groups of keywords.
  • A topic vector which represents the distribution of topics across documents. Each tweet has a topic vector containing five values, each corresponding to the probability of the tweet belonging to a specific topic.

A dominant topic is then assigned to each tweet. This is done simply by choosing the maximum value among the elements of the topic vector of each tweet.

The topic clusters are visualized using t-SNE and and K-means clustering.

  • t-SNE - this technique is applied to reduce the dimensionality of the topic vectors for visualization purposes
  • K-means clustering - this technique is applied to group the data points into clusters based on their similarities

Adjusted parameters: Parameters such as the maximum document frequency threshold and the number of topics and top words were adjusted to enhance the analysis. Specifically, having a maximum document frequency threshold of 85% means that words appearing more than 85% of the documents will be ignored. Furthermore, after performing numerous tests, five topics and 15 top words were chosen as the best parameters for identifying suitable and interpretable clusters.

The topic clusters are linked to Leni Robredo, with the majority of the tweets mentioning both Robredo and her foundation, Angat Buhay. These clusters also include allegations that either target or red-tag Robredo personally.

  • Cluster 1: The connection between Angat Buhay, Robredo, and the NPA
  • Cluster 2: Angat Buhay as a platform for NPA recruitment
  • Cluster 3: Angat Buhay and Robredo labeled as a Dilawan or a terrorist
  • Cluster 4: Perceptions of Angat Buhay as a dangerous foundation
  • Cluster 5: Discussions related to donation and funds of Angat Buhay, and the foundation acting as a front of the NPA

Event Detection

Pre-processing

For event detection, the following pre-processing techniques (as discussed in the Data section) were applied to the dates in the Date posted column:

  • Ensuring formatting consistency
  • Daily binning of data

Peak and Change-point Detection

  • Peak detection - We used SciPy’s find_peaks function. Because peaks are evaluated by visual inspection, we finetuned the function’s parameters by first starting with a baseline (all parameters equal to 1). It turns out that the only parameter we needed to change in order to detect all peaks is the height parameter, which was given a value of 7.

  • Change-point detection - We used the Pelt (Pruned Exact Linear Time) algorithm for change-point detection via Python’s ruptures library. Its Pelt function has a penalty parameter which represents the cost associated with adding a change-point. We continually adjusted its value until the change points detected became aligned with the peaks in the data. The final penalty value is 0.6.

Result

Figure #21: Line plot showing detected peaks and change-points from the time-series of tweets posted.

Peaks were observed on May 15, July 6, and July 11, coinciding with significant events. These include:

  • May 13: Robredo announces plan to launch Angat Buhay
  • June 30: Inauguration of Marcos
  • July 1: Launching of Angat Buhay
  • July 4: Badoy's false claim on Angat Buhay - NPA alliance
  • July 9-12: Angat Buhay - DSWD relief goods false claims

The change points in May coincide with the initial announcement of the launching of Angat Buhay Foundation, while the change point on July 1 marks the official launch, giving rise to discussions that extend until July 19. This period encompasses various related events, including the inauguration of Marcos and his first month in office, the dissemination of misinformed claims by Badoy, and the distribution of relief goods in Ifugao.

Here's what we found out.

Several key aspects characterizing mis/disinformation tweets regarding Angat Buhay's alleged alliance to the CPP-NPA-NDF were determined in the study.

  • It revealed that anonymous accounts were more likely to spread mis/disinformation about Angat Buhay's alleged affiliation with the CPP-NPA-NDF.
  • Topic clusters confirmed the prevalence of claims connecting Angat Buhay to the CPP-NPA-NDF and targeting its founder, Leni Robredo.
  • Events surrounding the announcement and launch of Angat Buhay witnessed the spread of mis/disinformation. Other instances of mis/disinformation, regardless of their connection to the CPP-NPA-NDF, continued to contribute to the dissemination of tweets falsely associating Angat Buhay with the group.

In conclusion, the prevalence of social media mis/disinformation presents a significant threat to organizations such as the Angat Buhay Foundation. Nevertheless, our project serves as a stepping stone to demonstrate the potential of data science and machine learning in proactively addressing this issue.

Through the techniques we employed, several important insights emerged.

We discovered that anonymity on social media becomes a powerful tool for individuals to freely spread mis/disinformation, as it removes personal accountability. This highlights the need for measures to address anonymous accounts and promote responsible online behavior.

Our analysis on topic clustering revealed that successful mis/disinformation campaigns often revolve around connected narratives or topics. Understanding these thematic connections can help in identifying and countering such campaigns effectively.

Our time series analysis shed light on the efficient spread of mis/disinformation campaigns when strategically timed with relevant events or news concerning the target victim. This underscores the importance of vigilance during critical moments to minimize the impact of false narratives.

Through this, we experienced firsthand the remarkable influence of data science in advocating for truth by interpreting and analyzing data. We look forward to a future where data science and machine learning empower the public in fighting mis/disinformation, thus safeguarding the truth in the ever-evolving digital landscape.
